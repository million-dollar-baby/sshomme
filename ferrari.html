POISE AND PRANCE

How do you make something that is already awesome even more appealing? Especially, if it has been accepted by the masses for eighty years. Well, that’s exactly what Ferrari has done with its all-new California T. The California has been Ferrari’s smallest and easiest car to drive and it continues to remain that way.
The differentiating factor with this model is the fact that the Italian experts have turned the design into a daring pantomime and made the engine smaller to 3855cc and added two crazy turbos to it allowing the power to go up to a whopping 560hp and 557 ft lbs of torque. I am certain that it is the correct way to improve upon a phenomenal car.

FThe Ferrari stands true to its tradition of flamboyance. The hard top convertible folds up beautifully completing the lines of the car showing its head turning beauty. The panels and the intakes flow in a way that makes it look like the car is in motion even when standing still and the noise it creates on firing it up and moving through the track is amazing! SS HOMME’s bespoke denim suit makes a dapper style statement. It translates from day to evening wear effortlessly. This ensemble from the menswear label breaks free from the pre-conceived notion that denims cannot be worn for a ceremonial occassion. The silhouette is sharp with clean cuts and a peak lapel.

Much like the suit, the simplicity of the car impresses me. It’s designed for everyday use. It can run around the city and cruise through the curvy mountain roads. It even comes with a cup-holder! The California T, though poised, as it seems, can prance around the racetrack putting other cars to shame.
